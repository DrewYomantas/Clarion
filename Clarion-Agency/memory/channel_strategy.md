# Clarion — Where Agents Should Focus External Activity

---

## Primary Channels

LinkedIn
  Why: Managing partners and legal ops professionals are active here.
  Use for: Authority content, company page, direct outreach angles, community participation.
  Content type: Short observations, governance insights, practitioner-tone posts.
  Engagement mode: Comment on relevant discussions. Post 2-4x/week once presence is established.

---

## Secondary Channels

Reddit (r/Lawyertalk, r/LawFirm, r/SmallLawFirm, r/AttorneyAdvice)
  Why: Real practitioners discuss real problems here candidly.
  Use for: Listening and discovery primarily. Occasional helpful participation.
  Content type: Direct, practical replies. No promotion. No links unless directly useful.
  Engagement mode: Helpful presence only. Never lead with Clarion.

Legal-specific forums and communities (Above the Law, Legal Talk Network, LMA)
  Why: Legal ops and marketing professionals — adjacent to the buyer.
  Use for: Discovery signals, occasional educational participation.
  Engagement mode: Low frequency. Educational only.

---

## Experimental Channels

Twitter / X
  Status: Monitor only until LinkedIn presence is established.
  Rationale: ICP is present but LinkedIn is higher-signal for B2B law firm buyers.

Newsletter / email (owned list)
  Status: Build list before sending. No outbound campaigns until CEO approves.

---

## Channels to Avoid

- TikTok, Instagram, YouTube — ICP not present in a buying context
- Generic SEO content farms — attracts wrong audience
- Broad legal news aggregators — low conversion intent
- Any channel requiring significant production resources before product-market fit

---

## Engagement Unlock Status

Phase 1 (current): Profile and content foundation — internal drafts only, no posting
Phase 2: Posting on approved channels — requires CEO approval per post or batch
Phase 3: Community engagement / commenting — requires Phase 2 complete + CEO unlock
Phase 4: Direct outreach — requires validated messaging + CEO approval
