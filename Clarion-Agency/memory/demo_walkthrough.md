# Clarion — Founder Walkthrough Guide
# Version: 1.0 | 2026-03-12

---

## Purpose

This is a structured founder-led walkthrough for prospects. It is not a
self-serve demo. It is a guided conversation that walks a managing partner
through what Clarion does, how it works, and ends with a concrete pilot offer.

Total time: 20–25 minutes.
Format: screen share or verbal description — no login required.

---

## The Walkthrough

---

### Step 1 — Problem Framing (3–4 minutes)

Open with:

  "Most law firms collect client feedback — reviews on Google or Avvo, intake
  notes, the occasional survey — but there's no structured way for leadership
  to read across all of it. It stays fragmented. Patterns that should inform
  how the firm operates stay invisible until they surface as a problem."

Probe questions (use 1–2 depending on conversation):
- "How does your leadership currently review client feedback?"
- "If a service delivery issue was building across multiple clients, how
  would you know?"
- "Do you ever look at your public reviews and wonder what the underlying
  pattern actually is?"

Listen for: reactive posture (waiting for complaints), no structured process,
partner visibility gap. These are the strongest buying signals.

---

### Step 2 — Feedback Ingestion (2 minutes)

  "Clarion takes client feedback as input — whether that's a CSV of public
  reviews or an export of your intake notes — and processes it to find
  what's actually there."

  "The input can be as simple as your last 30 Google reviews. You don't
  need to build anything or change your process."

Key point: No integration required for a pilot. Public reviews are enough.

---

### Step 3 — Theme Detection (3 minutes)

  "The first thing Clarion does is detect recurring themes. Not by keyword
  search — it reads the actual language of each review and groups feedback
  by what clients are actually saying."

  "Common themes we see in consumer-facing practices: communication gaps,
  timeline expectations, billing clarity, staff interaction quality. These
  show up at different frequencies and severities. Some are one-offs. Some
  are patterns."

  "The point isn't to count reviews. It's to find what's repeating."

---

### Step 4 — Signal Detection (3 minutes)

  "Beyond themes, Clarion flags governance signals — patterns in the
  feedback that rise to the level of something leadership should act on."

  "A signal is different from a theme. A theme is 'clients mention
  communication frequently.' A signal is 'communication issues are
  appearing in intake reviews and in one-star reviews at a rising rate
  over the last 90 days.'"

  "These are the things that end up in partner meetings — or should."

---

### Step 5 — Partner Action Recommendations (2 minutes)

  "Based on detected themes and signals, Clarion generates recommended
  partner actions. Not generic suggestions — specific actions tied to
  what the feedback actually shows."

  "For example: 'Three clients in the past quarter described unclear
  billing timelines. Recommended action: review intake communication
  template for billing expectation language.'"

  "Leadership gets something they can act on, not a report they file away."

---

### Step 6 — Governance Brief Output (2 minutes)

  "Everything is packaged into a governance brief — a clean, partner-ready
  document that shows the feedback summary, detected signals, and
  recommended actions. Designed for leadership review, not for an analyst
  to dig through."

  "The brief can be emailed to firm leadership on a schedule so it
  becomes a regular part of how the firm monitors client experience."

---

### Step 7 — Pilot Offer (3–4 minutes)

  "The easiest way to make this concrete is to run it on your actual data."

  "If you want, I can pull a sample of your public client reviews — 20–30
  from Google or Avvo — run them through the analysis, and share what comes
  out. No commitment. No setup on your side. Just a real output from real
  data so you can see whether this is useful."

  "Would that be worth doing before we talk next?"

If yes: move to pilot_execution_workflow.md and log as pilot_offered in pipeline.
If not yet: leave the offer open, log as interested, schedule follow-up.

## Objection Responses

"We already track reviews manually."
  → "That's a good foundation. What Clarion adds is the structural read —
    the patterns across reviews that are hard to see one at a time."

"We don't have time for another tool."
  → "There's nothing for you to set up or maintain. The pilot is one
    direction of output — I pull the data and share what I find."

"We're happy with our current reviews."
  → "That's useful context. Even firms with strong ratings often have
    signals in the lower-rated reviews that are worth knowing about at
    the partner level."

"What does it cost?"
  → Escalate to FOUNDER ESCALATIONS immediately. Do not quote pricing
    without CEO involvement.
