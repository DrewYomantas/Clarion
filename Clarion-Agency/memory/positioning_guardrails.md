# positioning_guardrails.md
# Clarion — Positioning Rules
# All market-facing agents must follow these rules in every output.

---

## Clarion's Wedge

Clarion operationalizes client feedback for leadership review.

The core value: managing partners get structured visibility into what clients
actually think — without having to chase it, read every review, or wait for
a complaint to escalate.

---

## Category Framing

Use this language:
- Client experience governance
- Feedback intelligence
- Operational intelligence for law firm leadership
- Governance brief
- Partner visibility

Avoid this language:
- Reputation management
- Review monitoring
- Social listening
- Client satisfaction platform
- AI-powered anything (scoring is deterministic; LLM is used for classification only)

---

## Messaging Rule

Lead with managing-partner outcomes, not features.

Good: "Partners know what clients are saying before it becomes a problem."
Bad: "Clarion uses AI to analyze your reviews and produce reports."

Good: "A governance brief your partners will actually read."
Bad: "Clarion's dashboard gives you actionable insights into your feedback."

---

## Positioning Drift Traps

Do not drift into these categories even loosely:
- Generic SaaS growth commentary
- Broad legal tech writing with no Clarion-specific angle
- Generic "AI for law firms" framing
- Reputation management positioning
- Practice management software
- Client intake or CRM tooling

If content could apply equally to any legal tech product, it is too generic.
Anchor every piece to the specific managing-partner pain Clarion addresses.

---

## Differentiation Points

Distinguish Clarion from:
- Reputation monitoring tools: Clarion doesn't just track — it structures and governs
- Review dashboards: Clarion produces governance briefs for leadership, not analytics screens
- Legal practice management platforms: Clarion is not operational software — it's intelligence

---

## Competitive Sensitivity

Do not name competitors in public-facing content unless CEO approves.
In internal analysis, name them clearly. In external content, describe the category gap.
