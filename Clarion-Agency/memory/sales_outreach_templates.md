---

## Messaging Principles

Lead with: client feedback blind spots, hidden patterns in reviews, partner-level visibility
Avoid: generic legal tech language, AI hype claims, reputation management framing
Tone: knowledgeable practitioner, not vendor

---

## Template 1 — Cold Email

Subject: Client feedback patterns at [Firm Name]

---

Hi [First Name],

Most law firms collect client reviews but don't have a structured way to read them
at the partner level. Feedback stays fragmented — and the patterns that matter
only surface after a problem becomes visible.

Clarion converts your public client reviews into a concise governance brief —
themes, sentiment patterns, and recommended partner actions — so leadership has
visibility before issues escalate.

If you have 20 minutes, I'd be glad to walk you through how it works using a
sample of your actual public reviews.

[Name]

---

Adapt for:
- Pilot reference variant: "We reviewed a sample of [Firm Name]'s public
  client feedback and noticed a few patterns that may be worth discussing."
- Follow-up variant: see Template 3.

## Template 2 — LinkedIn Message

---

Hi [First Name] — I work with law firms that are trying to get better structured
visibility into client feedback at the partner level.

Most of the feedback firms collect ends up in review platforms or intake notes,
never in a format leadership can actually act on.

Clarion converts public reviews into a short governance brief — themes,
sentiment signals, and suggested partner actions. Happy to show you how it works
in 20 minutes using a sample of your firm's reviews if it sounds relevant.

---

Keep to 3 sentences if the firm has not engaged before.
Longer message only on second contact or if there's a strong signal.

---

## Template 3 — Follow-Up Message

---

Hi [First Name],

Following up briefly — I wanted to make sure my earlier note reached you.

If now isn't the right time, no problem. But if client feedback visibility
is something you're thinking about, even a quick look at a pilot analysis
of your reviews might be useful context.

Happy to share more or set up 20 minutes when timing works.

[Name]

---

Rules for follow-up:
- Send only once after no reply (not twice)
- Wait at least 7 days after initial outreach
- Do not repeat the same framing verbatim

---

## Template 4 — Pilot Offer (after initial contact)

---

Hi [First Name],

To make this concrete rather than abstract — I can run a short analysis of
[Firm Name]'s public client reviews and share what patterns come out.

This would cover detected feedback themes, sentiment signals, and a few
suggested areas for partner attention. Takes 20–50 reviews and produces a
one-page brief. No commitment involved — it's just a way to make the
conversation more useful.

Would that be worth doing before we talk?

[Name]

---

Use only when:
- Prospect has responded positively to initial outreach
- Reviews are publicly available
- Pilot is pre-approved or can be run quickly
