## PROBLEM

Managing partners at small and mid-size law firms receive feedback from clients
constantly — through reviews, intake surveys, post-matter messages, and informal
channels. None of it is structured. None of it surfaces as a governance signal.

The result: problems that clients are signaling repeatedly go unaddressed until
they become complaints, departures, or reputation damage. Partners don't know
what their clients are actually saying. They're running on anecdote, not evidence.

The problem is not a lack of feedback. It is a lack of visibility.

---

## WHO CLARION IS FOR

Managing partners and firm leadership at small to mid-size law firms (typically 1–50
attorneys) who are responsible for client experience, firm reputation, and operational
quality — but who do not have a dedicated operations or analytics function.

They are time-constrained. They trust structured information over raw data dumps.
They need something they can review in a partner meeting, not a dashboard to learn.

Clarion is NOT for:
- Solo practitioners with no team to govern
- Large firms with dedicated client experience or data teams
- Any business outside the legal vertical

---

## WHAT CLARION DOES

Clarion takes fragmented client feedback — reviews, surveys, intake responses,
post-matter messages — and turns it into a structured governance brief that a
managing partner can act on.

Step by step:
1. Feedback is uploaded as a CSV or collected from connected sources
2. Clarion classifies each item into governance themes using LLM classification
   at temperature 0.0 — deterministic, not probabilistic
3. Signals are extracted: which themes are recurring, which are escalating,
   which require a partner-level response
4. A branded governance brief is generated as a PDF, with action items
5. The brief is delivered by email on a scheduled basis or on demand

The output a managing partner receives is a governance brief — readable,
structured, meeting-ready. Not a dashboard. Not a chart. A document they can
put in front of the partnership.

---

## WHAT SIGNALS MEAN

A governance signal is a pattern in client feedback that points to a
firm-management issue — not just an individual client's opinion.

Examples:
- Three clients in six weeks mention feeling uninformed about billing — that is
  a communication pattern, not a one-off complaint
- Multiple clients reference waiting too long for updates — that is a responsiveness
  signal that warrants a process review
- Positive feedback clustering around one attorney and neutral-to-negative clustering
  around another — that is a performance signal

Clarion's classification maps feedback to governance themes, not sentiment scores.
The signal tells a managing partner what to do, not just how people feel.

## WHAT ACTIONS PRODUCE

A completed Clarion analysis produces:
- A categorized view of feedback by governance theme
- Identified signals with frequency and trend direction
- Recommended partner actions tied to specific themes
- A governance brief PDF ready for partner review or file
- Email delivery on schedule or on demand

---

## WHY GOVERNANCE MATTERS

"Governance" is the right frame for this problem because the managing partner's
job is not to read every review — it is to ensure the firm is operating in a way
that serves clients consistently.

Client feedback governance means: the firm has a structured, repeatable process
for turning client signals into management decisions.

Clarion is the infrastructure for that process.
Without it, governance is anecdote. With it, governance is evidence.

---

## WHAT MAKES CLARION DIFFERENT

Clarion is not a review monitoring tool. It does not watch star ratings or alert
on negative sentiment. It classifies feedback into governance themes and produces
a structured brief that a partner can act on.

The differentiating properties:
- Governance brief as the output, not a dashboard
- LLM classification at deterministic temperature, not keyword matching
- Built for leadership decision-making, not operational tracking
- Law firm-specific theme taxonomy, not generic feedback categories
- Delivers to email — no login required to receive value

What Clarion is not:
- Not reputation management
- Not a review response tool
- Not a client intake or CRM system
- Not a generic analytics platform
- Not a practice management tool
