# Clarion — Social Posting Cadence & Authenticity Guide
# All agents producing social content must read this file before drafting or scheduling posts.
# Also read: memory/brand_voice.md and memory/external_interaction_policy.md

---

## Core Principle

Clarion's social presence should feel like a knowledgeable practitioner sharing
useful things — not a content machine on a schedule. The moment posting looks
automated, the credibility it was meant to build starts to erode.

Volume and consistency are not goals. Relevance and quality are.

---

## Posting Cadence

### LinkedIn
- **Target:** 2–4 posts per week
- **Do not** post at the same time every day
- **Do not** post on the same days every week — vary the pattern
- Occasionally skip a day or two without replacement content
- A quiet week followed by two strong posts is better than five weak ones

### Twitter / X
- **Target:** 3–6 posts per week
- Mix short observations, responses to relevant discussions, and standalone content
- Replies and retweets of genuinely relevant content count — they are not filler
- Do not manufacture activity to hit a number

### Community Participation (Reddit, forums, LinkedIn groups, Slack communities)
- Participate when there is something genuinely useful to contribute
- Do not participate on a schedule
- Frequency should feel contextual — some weeks active, some weeks quiet
- One high-quality comment in a real discussion is worth more than ten generic ones

## Authenticity Rules for All Social Content

These rules apply to every post, comment, reply, and caption drafted by any agent.

### Sentence structure
- Vary sentence length — short sentences after longer ones land harder
- Do not open three consecutive sentences with the same word or structure
- Do not use the same opening pattern in posts published close together
  (e.g., two posts in a row starting with a rhetorical question, or with "Most firms...")

### Voice
- Write like an experienced operator explaining something to a colleague
- Assume the reader is intelligent and experienced — do not over-explain
- Educational tone first; promotional tone only when it is genuinely warranted
- If a post reads like marketing copy, rewrite it as a useful observation

### Language
- No exaggerated marketing language (see `memory/brand_voice.md` prohibited phrases)
- No AI-phrasing patterns: "In today's X landscape", "It's more important than ever",
  "Unlock the power of", "This is a game-changer"
- No forced enthusiasm: "Excited to share", "Thrilled to announce", "So pumped about"
- No hollow openers: "Great question", "Absolutely", "Certainly"

### Format variety
- Vary post format week to week: plain text, short observation, question, list,
  brief story, stat + context, contrarian take
- Do not use the same format in back-to-back posts
- Avoid bullet-heavy posts for every piece of content — prose reads as more human

## AI-Pattern Detection — Agent Self-Check

Before submitting any batch of drafts, the producing agent must review them
against these signals. If any pattern is present, revise before surfacing.

**Suspicious patterns to catch before they ship:**

- Posts proposed at identical times across multiple days
- Two or more posts with the same opening sentence structure in the same batch
- Every post in a batch following the same format (e.g., all bullet lists, all
  rhetorical question openers, all "Most firms..." starters)
- Three or more consecutive drafts leading with a product mention or CTA
- Identical sign-off or closing structure repeated across posts
- A burst of 3+ posts proposed for the same day without a conversation trigger
- Every post in a batch using identical length (all short, or all long)
- Language patterns that read like a template was filled in:
  "[Observation]. [Implication]. [Clarion connection]."

If you detect any of these in your own output: stop, revise, and vary before
including the drafts in the report. Do not flag it as an escalation — fix it.

## Warning Signs (Chief of Staff monitors for these)

The following patterns signal that posting has drifted into machine-like behavior
and must be flagged in the CEO brief:

| Pattern | Threshold for flagging |
|---|---|
| Overly regular cadence | Same posting days/times 3+ weeks in a row |
| Overly frequent | LinkedIn >5 posts/week or Twitter >8 posts/week sustained |
| Repetitive structure | Same sentence opening or format 3+ consecutive posts |
| Volume over quality | Multiple posts in one day with thin substance |
| Promotional drift | 3+ consecutive posts with no educational value |

When any pattern is detected, flag it under SOCIAL HEALTH in the CEO brief.

**Cadence health status labels (use in SOCIAL HEALTH section):**

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Healthy** | No patterns detected. Cadence varies. Content is substantive and educational. |
| **Drifting** | One pattern detected. Flag it. Instruct Comms & Content to adjust next cycle. |
| **Concerning** | Two or more patterns detected, or the same pattern persists two weeks running. Escalate in CEO brief. |

## What Good Looks Like

A natural week might look like:
- Monday: nothing
- Tuesday: a LinkedIn post — a short observation from a law firm feedback insight
- Wednesday: a Twitter reply to something relevant in legal tech
- Thursday: nothing
- Friday: a LinkedIn post — a contrarian take on client review management
- Weekend: nothing

The week has variety, gaps, and no artificial padding.
The posts don't sound like each other. Neither feels scheduled.
